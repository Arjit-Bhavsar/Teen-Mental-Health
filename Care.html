Self-Care Strategies

Learn how to take care of your physical and mental health through daily habits that actually work.

What is Self-Care?

Self-care is the practice of taking deliberate actions to care for your physical, mental, and emotional health. It decreases stress, anxiety, and depression symptoms while boosting your overall wellbeing.

Self-care is not about bubble baths and face masks (though those can be nice). It is about making choices that help you function at your best--getting enough sleep, eating well, moving your body, and taking care of your mind.

Think of self-care as maintenance for your body and brain. Just like you charge your phone every day, you need to recharge yourself too.

Mental Self-Care Strategies

Practical ways to take care of your mental health every day.

Set Clear Intentions

Decide on one small thing each day: say something positive about yourself, do one thing that makes you happy, or talk to a friend when you are feeling down.

Connect with Others

Deepen connections with friends and family. Studies show social connections enhance mood and even decrease mortality rates.

Practice Relaxation

Activate your parasympathetic nervous system through deep breathing, visualizing a calm place, or progressively relaxing muscle groups.

Turn Emotions into Actions

When you feel sad, take positive action--listen to upbeat music, talk with a friend, or do something that brings you joy.

Why Sleep Matters

Sleep is essential for teen development. It allows for muscle rebuilding and supports bodily functions like focus, memory, and emotional regulation. Teens should sleep 9-10 hours each night.

During sleep, your brain processes what you learned during the day, your body repairs itself, and your immune system strengthens. Skipping sleep does not give you more time--it just makes you less effective at everything.

Tips for better sleep: keep a consistent bedtime, avoid screens an hour before bed, keep your room cool and dark, and limit caffeine after noon.

Balancing School and Life

Practical strategies to manage your time without burning out.

1

Create a Schedule

Use a calendar to block off classes and activities, then schedule time for things that matter to you--reading, walking, playing with pets, or just relaxing.

2

Learn to Say No

Say no to things you do not want to do. It could be a concert you will not enjoy or spending too much time with others when you need space. Your time is valuable.

3

Accept Imperfection

You are human. You will not get everything done in a day, and that is okay. Give yourself grace and focus on progress, not perfection.
